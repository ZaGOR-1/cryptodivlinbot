# Privacy Policy — cryptodivlinbot

_Last updated: 2026-04-26_

This document explains what data the **cryptodivlinbot** Telegram bot
collects, why, and how you can have it deleted. The bot is open-source —
the code that handles your data is at
<https://github.com/ZaGOR-1/cryptodivlinbot> and you are encouraged to
review it.

## 1. Who runs this bot?

The bot is operated by the deployer who runs the source code on their
own infrastructure. If you are reaching this policy from a Telegram chat
with a specific bot account (for example `@cryptodivlin_bot`), the
operator is the owner of that bot account. They are the **data
controller** within the meaning of the EU General Data Protection
Regulation (GDPR).

If you do not know who runs the instance you're talking to, you can ask
in the chat — the operator is required to identify themselves.

## 2. What data the bot stores

The bot stores a **minimal** amount of personal data, just enough to
make the bot work for you. All data is kept locally in a SQLite
database; nothing is sold, traded, or shared with third parties.

| Data | Source | Purpose | Retention |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Telegram **chat id** | Sent automatically by Telegram on every message | Identify the chat to deliver alerts and digests to | Until you ask for deletion or unsubscribe |
| **Interface language** (`en`, `uk`, `ru`) | You pick it via `/language` or `/setlang` | Render messages in the language you prefer | Same as chat id |
| **Spike threshold** override | You set it via `/setthreshold` | Personalize how sensitive alerts are for you | Same as chat id |
| **Subscription state** | Toggled by `/subscribe` and `/unsubscribe` | Decide whether to send alerts to your chat | Same as chat id |
| **Last-alert timestamps per coin** | Generated by the bot when it sends an alert | Enforce per-chat / per-coin alert cool-downs (no spam) | 24 hours rolling |

The bot **does not** store:

- Your Telegram username, first name, last name, phone number, or
  profile picture.
- Message content other than the slash command you sent.
- Your IP address.
- Any analytics, advertising, or tracking identifiers.

## 3. Cryptocurrency price history

The bot also stores a rolling 24-hour window of **public** market
prices for the top-N coins by market cap. This data is not personal
information — it is the same data anyone can fetch from CoinGecko or
Binance. It exists only to compute spike percentages over the
configured window.

## 4. Backups

The operator may run the bot's automatic backup feature, which writes
periodic snapshots of the SQLite database to disk (configured via
`BACKUP_DIR` and rotated to keep at most `BACKUP_RETENTION_COUNT`
files). Snapshots contain the same data as the live database — no more,
no less.

If backups are stored off-machine (e.g. an S3 bucket), the operator is
responsible for transit security.

## 5. How long data is kept

- **As long as you keep the chat with the bot active** for chat
  preferences (id, language, threshold, subscription).
- **24 hours** for price history, then automatically pruned.
- **Cooldown timestamps** are overwritten on each new alert and
  effectively expire after `ALERT_COOLDOWN_MIN`.

## 6. Your rights

Under GDPR (and equivalent laws elsewhere) you have the right to:

- **Access** the data the bot has about you. Send `/status` and
  `/coins` to see your current settings; the operator can also export
  your raw rows on request.
- **Correct** any of it — change language with `/setlang`, change
  threshold with `/setthreshold`.
- **Delete** all data the bot has about your chat. Send `/forgetme` —
  the bot will purge every row tied to your chat id (subscription,
  language, threshold, cooldown timestamps) and stop messaging you.
  This is irreversible.
- **Withdraw consent** by sending `/unsubscribe` (stops alerts but
  keeps your preferences) or `/forgetme` (full deletion).
- **Lodge a complaint** with your local data-protection authority. In
  the EU, that is the supervisory authority of your country of
  residence.

## 7. Children

This bot is not directed at children under 13 (or under 16 in some EU
jurisdictions). If you are a parent or guardian and believe a child
under that age has used the bot, send `/forgetme` from their chat.

## 8. Security

The bot runs on the operator's infrastructure. You should evaluate
their security claims directly. The source code uses only well-known,
audited libraries (`python-telegram-bot`, `httpx`, SQLite stdlib) and
keeps the database in WAL mode for crash safety.

## 9. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the operator will note it in the
GitHub repository's `CHANGELOG.md` and update the "Last updated" date
at the top of this file. Continued use of the bot after the update
constitutes acceptance.

## 10. Contact

For data-subject requests or questions, contact the bot operator
directly via the Telegram chat with the bot, or open an issue on the
public repository at
<https://github.com/ZaGOR-1/cryptodivlinbot/issues>.
